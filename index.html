About Us

What is a Bloco?

A bloco (block) is a Brazilian Portuguese word for a community percussion ensemble with roots in Afro-Brazilian Carnival processions. Similar to Mardi Gras second-line bands, blocos parade in the streets as large drum ensembles that manifest an effervescent fusion of arts and culture, including music, dance, costumes, Carnival themes, and activism.

To respectfully build upon these Afro-Brazilian roots of rhythmic resistance, we aim to unite the community against hate and racial discrimination through the unity, music, rhythm, and performance of Bloco GNV.

Our Music

Rhythms of the African Diaspora

Bloco GNV plays a diverse variety of rhythms respectfully blended from Brazil, the USA, the Caribbean, and Africa. Our repertoire includes Carnival music like samba-reggae and samba-afro from Salvador, Brazil, second-line Mardi Gras arrangements, African and Afro-Caribbean rhythms, and our own rhythmic creations.

We aim to underscore the unique and brilliant experience of playing Afro-diasporic rhythms. No prior music experience is necessary. We encourage you to attend weekly classes and be serious about learning your parts.
