About Us

What is a Bloco?

A bloco (block) is a Brazilian Portuguese word for a community percussion ensemble with roots in Afro-Brazilian Carnaval processions. Similar to Mardi Gras second-line bands, blocos parade in the streets as large drum ensembles that manifest an effervescent fusion of arts and culture, including music, dance, costumes, Carnaval themes, and activism.

To respectfully build upon these Afro-Brazilian roots of rhythmic resistance, we unite the community through music, rhythm, and performance of Bloco GNV.

Our Music

Rhythms of the African Diaspora

Bloco GNV plays a diverse variety of rhythms respectfully blended from Brazil, the USA, the Caribbean, and Africa. Our repertoire includes Carnaval music like samba-reggae and samba-afro from Salvador, Brazil, second-line Mardi Gras arrangements, African and Afro-Caribbean rhythms, and our own rhythmic creations.

We underscore the unique and brilliant experience of playing Afro-diasporic rhythms. No prior music experience is necessary. We encourage you to attend weekly classes.
